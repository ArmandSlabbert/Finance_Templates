# Balance Sheet Reconciliation Checklist

## Overview
This Balance Sheet Reconciliation Checklist is a structured control tool designed to ensure the accuracy, completeness, and integrity of balance sheet accounts as part of the month-end close process.

It supports Finance Managers, Financial Accountants, and Controllers in performing systematic reconciliations, identifying discrepancies early, and maintaining audit-ready documentation.

---

## Purpose
The checklist is designed to:
- Ensure all balance sheet accounts are reconciled monthly
- Detect and resolve errors, omissions, or misstatements
- Strengthen financial controls and governance
- Support internal and external audit requirements
- Provide clear accountability and sign-off

---

## Scope
This checklist applies to all balance sheet accounts, including but not limited to:
- Cash and bank accounts
- VAT and tax control accounts
- Trade and other receivables
- Trade and other payables
- Accruals and prepayments
- Provisions
- Fixed assets and depreciation
- Intercompany and loan accounts
- Equity accounts

---

## Key Controls
- All balance sheet accounts reconciled monthly
- Reconciliations supported by independent evidence
- Aged balances reviewed and explained
- Differences investigated and resolved promptly
- Review and approval documented
- Reconciliations retained for audit purposes

---

## Checklist Structure
The checklist includes:
- Account name and GL reference
- Opening balance
- Movement for the period
- Closing balance per GL
- Reconciliation status (Completed / Outstanding)
- Supporting documentation reference
- Preparer sign-off
- Reviewer sign-off
- Review notes and follow-up actions

---

## Frequency
- **Monthly** as part of the month-end close process
- **Ad-hoc** for audit, due diligence, or management review

---

## Ownership & Accountability
- **Prepared by:** Financial Accountant / Bookkeeper  
- **Reviewed by:** Finance Manager / Financial Controller  
- **Approved by:** Finance Manager / Director (where applicable)
