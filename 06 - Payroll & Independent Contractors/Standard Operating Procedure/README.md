## Overview
This directory contains the official Standard Operating Procedure governing payroll processing and the management of independent contractors.

The objective is to ensure remuneration is:

- accurate  
- compliant  
- timely  
- correctly classified  
- supported by documentation  

Payroll is a high-risk area affecting employees, regulators, and company reputation.

---

## What This SOP Covers
The procedure establishes mandatory controls for:

- Employee and contractor setup  
- Payroll calculations  
- Statutory deductions (PAYE, UIF, SDL)  
- Payroll reviews  
- Contractor classification  
- Payments and submissions  
- Reconciliations  
- Record retention  

It applies to **all permanent employees, temporary staff, and contractors**.

## Control Principles Embedded in This SOP

### 1) Legal Compliance
Payroll must comply with labour and tax legislation.

### 2) Accurate Master Data
Incorrect employee data leads to incorrect pay.

### 3) Review Before Pay
No payroll should run without validation.

### 4) Proper Classification
Contractor vs employee risk must be assessed.

### 5) Monthly Reconciliation
Payroll must tie back to the GL and statutory balances.

---

## Core Activities in This SOP

### Employee Governance
- Maintain signed employment contracts  
- ensure payroll master data integrity  
- approve remuneration changes  

### Payroll Processing
- Calculate earnings and deductions  
- apply PAYE, UIF, SDL  
- produce payroll reports  

### Review & Approval
- Validate accuracy and reasonableness  
- clear discrepancies before payment  
- Finance Manager approval  

### Contractors
- confirm agreements  
- assess misclassification risk  
- apply withholding taxes where required  

### Statutory Compliance
- submit returns by deadlines  
- arrange payments to authorities  

### Reconciliations
- payroll expense vs GL  
- PAYE/UIF/SDL control accounts  
- headcount and variance reviews
