## Overview
This directory contains the official Standard Operating Procedure governing VAT calculation, reconciliation, submission, and payment.

The objective is to ensure VAT reporting is:

- accurate  
- complete  
- compliant with legislation  
- supported by reconciliations  
- defensible under audit  

VAT is a high-visibility statutory obligation and errors can create financial, legal, and director risk.

---

## What This SOP Covers
The procedure establishes mandatory controls for:

- VAT output and input tax determination  
- Valid tax invoice requirements  
- Periodic VAT reconciliations  
- Submission of VAT returns  
- Payment of liabilities  
- Record retention  
- Review and approval responsibilities  

It applies to **all VAT-registered activities and transactions**.

## Control Principles Embedded in This SOP

### 1) Legislative Compliance
All VAT treatment must comply with applicable tax law and guidance.

### 2) Invoice Validity
Input tax may only be claimed on compliant supplier documentation.

### 3) Reconciliation Discipline
VAT returns must tie to accounting records.

### 4) Timely Submission
Returns and payments must meet statutory deadlines.

### 5) Independent Review
VAT calculations require oversight before filing.

---

## Core Activities in This SOP

### VAT Determination
- Identify output VAT on sales  
- confirm input VAT eligibility  
- treat zero-rated and exempt supplies correctly  

### Return Preparation
- Compile VAT reports from accounting systems  
- prepare reconciliations between GL and return  

### Review & Approval
- Investigate unusual movements  
- confirm completeness and accuracy  
- Finance Manager approval before submission  

### Payment & Filing
- Submit returns to the tax authority  
- arrange settlement of liabilities  

### Documentation
- Maintain reconciliations, invoices, and working papers  
- ensure availability for audit
