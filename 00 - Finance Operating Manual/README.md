## Why This Manual Exists
The Finance Operating Manual is the central control framework for how
finance activities are executed, reviewed, and evidenced.

It converts accounting requirements into repeatable operational
processes that management and auditors can rely on.

---

## How To Use This Manual
Users should:

1. Identify the relevant process area  
2. Follow the applicable SOP  
3. Prepare evidence as required  
4. Obtain the defined review and approval  
5. Escalate exceptions where necessary  

If an activity is not documented or supported, it is considered incomplete.

---

## Authority of the SOPs
The procedures within this manual represent the approved method of
working.

Deviations must be:
- documented  
- justified  
- approved by Finance Management  

Unapproved deviations may lead to audit findings or compliance risk.

---

## Who Relies On This Manual
This framework supports:

- Finance staff executing daily activities  
- Reviewers performing oversight  
- Management relying on reported numbers  
- Auditors assessing control effectiveness  
- Regulators reviewing compliance  

---

## What Makes This Framework Strong
The manual is built on principles of:

- segregation of duties  
- documented evidence  
- repeatability  
- transparency  
- accountability  

---

## Relationship To Other Documentation
This manual works together with:

- Delegation of authority  
- Accounting policies  
- Risk management frameworks  
- Audit plans  
- Regulatory requirements  

---

## Change Governance
Updates to processes must:

- be formally documented  
- include rationale for change  
- be approved  
- be version controlled  

---

## Signs the Manual Is Working Well
You should see:

- fewer surprises at period end  
- faster reviews  
- clear ownership of issues  
- reduced audit queries  
- consistent outputs
